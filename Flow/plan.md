# Unified Health Manager - Architecture Plan

This document outlines the detailed Entity-Relationship (ER) structure for the Unified Health Manager system, including core entities, their attributes, and the relationships connecting them.

---

## 1. The Core Users (Entities & Attributes)
These are the people and organizations interacting with the system.

* **Patient** (The user of the system)
  * *Attributes:* `id` (Unique identifier), `name`, `cloud_access` (Personalized cloud-based account).
* **General DOCTOR** (The General Practitioner overseeing care)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `rx_oversight` (Prescription oversight status), `No_schedule_hassle` (Indicates asynchronous review capabilities).
* **Specialist** (Specialized doctors handling tougher cases)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `complex_cases` (Flag for handling complex cases).
* **Pharma** (The pharmacy or pharmacists)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `branch_name`.

---

## 2. Health Data and Tracking
Entities storing the medical information and automated system warnings.

* **health_record** (The central repository for a patient's health data)
  * *Attributes:* `internal_id`, `anony_id` (Anonymized ID so doctors can review securely), `online_report`.
* **diagnostics** (Stores specific test results)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `blood_pressure`, `USG` (Ultrasound), `X-ray`.
* **recommendation** (Actionable advice generated by the system)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `diet_warning`, `test_freq` (Recommended frequency for medical tests).
* **medical_flag** (Serious alerts raised by doctors or the system)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `combo_danger` (Alert for dangerous drug combinations), `EMS_required` (Alert for emergencies like strokes).

---

## 3. Medication and Pharmacy Operations
Entities handling the supply, cost, and tracking of drugs.

* **Medication** (The catalog of available drugs)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `genetic_type` (Generic equivalent), `duration` (Typical prescription length), `blackbox_warn` (Serious side effects), `stop_if` (Condition to halt use), `pop+price` (Algorithm score for the best cost/generic option).
* **Prescription** (The actual order for medicine)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `Instant_order` (Status flag).
* **Inventory** (Tracks what the pharmacy actually has in stock)
  * *Attributes:* `id`, `avail_stock` (Current shelf inventory), `demand` (Tracking metric), `dropship` (Option for delivery).

---

## 4. How Everything Connects (Relationships)

### Patient Interactions
* **1:1** - A **Patient** `owns` a **health_record**. (Direct link).
* **1:N** - A **Patient** `uploads` data to their **health_record**.

### Doctor Interactions
* **1:N** - A **General DOCTOR** `Reviews Monthly` multiple **health_records**.
* **1:N** - A **General DOCTOR** `Raises` multiple **medical_flags** if they spot a danger.
* **1:N** - A **Specialist** `reviews weekly` multiple **health_records**.
* **M:N** - A **Specialist** `confers with` other **Specialists** to solve complex cases.

### System Automations (The "Smart" Features)
* **1:N** - A **health_record** `contains` many **diagnostics**.
* **1:N** - A **health_record** `generates` many **recommendations** based on the data.
* **1:N** - A **Medication** can `Trigger warning` to create multiple **medical_flags** if a bad drug combination is detected.

### Pharmacy and Logistics
* **1:1** - A **Pharma** `Manages` their **Inventory**.
* **1:N** - A **Pharma** `fulfills` many **Prescriptions**.
* **1:N** - A **Pharma** `analyze Trends` across many anonymized **health_records** to predict demand.
* **1:N** - The **Inventory** `Tracks` multiple **Medications**.
* **M:N** - A **Prescription** connects to **Medication** through the `medicines` relationship (meaning a prescription can have multiple medicines, and a medicine belongs to multiple prescriptions).
